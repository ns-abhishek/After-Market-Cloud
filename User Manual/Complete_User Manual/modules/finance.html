Finance Module Overview

The Finance module is the central hub for all financial activities in your organization. It provides tools for managing accounting, financial reporting, budgeting, and financial analysis. This module integrates with other modules to provide a complete financial picture of your business operations.

Getting Started

If you're new to the Finance module, we recommend starting with the General Ledger section to understand the core financial structure, then exploring the other sections based on your specific needs.

General Ledger

The General Ledger is the foundation of your financial system, providing a complete record of all financial transactions. It serves as the central repository for all your accounting data and is essential for accurate financial reporting.

The General Ledger module includes the following key components:

  • Chart of Accounts - The organizational structure for all financial accounts
  • Journal Entries - Records of financial transactions
  • Account Reconciliation - Tools to verify account balances
  • Financial Periods - Management of accounting periods
  • Trial Balance - Summary of account balances

Accessing the General Ledger

Navigate to the General Ledger

From the main menu, select Finance > General Ledger. This will open the General Ledger dashboard with summary information.

Explore the Dashboard

The dashboard displays key financial metrics, recent transactions, and account balances. You can customize this view based on your preferences.

Use Filters for Specific Data

Use the filters at the top to narrow down the view by date range, account, or other criteria to focus on the information you need.

Chart of Accounts

The Chart of Accounts is a hierarchical structure of all accounts used in your financial system. It organizes your financial data into categories like assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, and expenses, making it easier to track and report on your financial activities.

  1. From the General Ledger dashboard, click on Chart of Accounts in the sidebar
  2. The system displays a hierarchical view of all accounts
  3. Click on any account to view its details and transaction history
  4. Use the search and filter options to find specific accounts
  5. You can expand or collapse account groups to see different levels of detail
  1. From the Chart of Accounts page, click the Add Account button
  2. Fill in the required fields:
    • Account Number
    • Account Name
    • Account Type (Asset, Liability, Equity, Revenue, Expense)
    • Parent Account (if applicable)
    • Description
  3. Click Save to create the account

Best Practice

Maintain a consistent numbering system for your accounts to make them easier to identify and organize. For example, use 1000-1999 for assets, 2000-2999 for liabilities, etc.

Journal Entries

Journal entries are used to record financial transactions in the General Ledger. They are the building blocks of your accounting system and ensure that your financial records are accurate and complete.

Understanding Debits and Credits

Every journal entry must balance, meaning the total debits must equal the total credits. This ensures that your accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) remains in balance.

Access Journal Entries

From the General Ledger dashboard, click on Journal Entries in the sidebar, then click the New Journal Entry button.

Enter Header Information

Fill in the header information including Date, Reference Number, Description, and Source (optional).

Add Line Items

Click Add Line to add transaction details. For each line, select an Account, enter Debit or Credit amount, add a description (optional), and select a department or cost center if applicable.

Verify and Post

Ensure that total debits equal total credits, then click Save to save as draft or Post to post the journal entry.

Important

Once a journal entry is posted, it cannot be deleted. If you need to reverse a posted entry, create a new journal entry with the opposite debits and credits.

Accounts Receivable

The Accounts Receivable module helps you manage customer invoices, payments, and credit management. It provides tools to track money owed to your business and monitor customer payment history.

Customer Management
Creating Invoices
Recording Payments

Customer Management

Before creating invoices, you need to set up customer records. This information will be used for invoicing, payment processing, and reporting.

Access Customer Management

Navigate to Finance > Accounts Receivable > Customers and click New Customer.

Enter Basic Information

Fill in the customer's name, ID (generated automatically or entered manually), and contact information.

Add Address Details

Enter the billing address and shipping address (if different) for the customer.

Set Financial Parameters

Specify payment terms, credit limit, and tax information for the customer.

Save the Customer Record

Review all information and click Save to create the customer record.

Creating Invoices

Invoices are documents that request payment from customers for products or services provided. Creating accurate and timely invoices is essential for maintaining healthy cash flow.

Interactive Guide: Creating an Invoice

Access Invoice Creation

Navigate to Finance > Accounts Receivable > Invoices and click New Invoice.

Select Customer and Enter Details

Select a customer from the dropdown and fill in the invoice details including Invoice Date, Due Date, Invoice Number, Reference Number (optional), and Payment Terms.

Add Line Items

Click Add Item to add products or services. For each item, select the product/service, enter quantity and price, add a description if needed, and select the applicable tax rate.

Apply Discounts or Charges

Add any discounts or additional charges that apply to the invoice.

Review and Finalize

Review the invoice total and click Save to save as draft or Post to finalize the invoice.

Invoice Status

Invoices can have different statuses:

  • Draft - Invoice is saved but not yet sent to the customer
  • Posted - Invoice is finalized and ready to be sent
  • Sent - Invoice has been sent to the customer
  • Partially Paid - Customer has made a partial payment
  • Paid - Invoice has been fully paid
  • Overdue - Payment deadline has passed without full payment

Recording Payments

When customers pay their invoices, you need to record these payments in the system to keep your accounts receivable up to date.

Payment Terms

Payment terms define when payment is due (e.g., Net 30 means payment is due 30 days after the invoice date).

Invoice Numbering

A systematic approach to assigning unique identifiers to invoices for easy tracking and reference.

Payment Methods

Different ways customers can pay (e.g., credit card, bank transfer, check).

Access Payment Recording

Navigate to Finance > Accounts Receivable > Payments and click New Payment.

Select Customer

Select the customer who made the payment from the dropdown menu.

Enter Payment Details

Enter the Payment Date, Payment Method, Reference Number (e.g., check number), and Amount.

Apply Payment to Invoices

Select the invoices to which the payment should be applied. The system will automatically allocate the payment amount to the selected invoices.

Post the Payment

Review the payment details and click Post Payment to record the transaction.

Pro Tip

You can set up automatic payment reminders for overdue invoices. Go to Finance > Settings > Payment Reminders to configure this feature.
